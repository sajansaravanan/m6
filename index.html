Cassiopeia A

Cassiopeia A is the remnant of a supernova explosion that occurred approximately 350 years ago.

It is located about 11,000 light-years from Earth in the constellation Cassiopeia.

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope has captured new images revealing intricate details of this supernova remnant.

The observations provide insights into the processes of star death and the distribution of elements in the universe.

Cassiopeia A remains a key object of study for understanding the life cycle of massive stars.
